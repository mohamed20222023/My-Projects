From the garage to the Google headquarters

The Google story began in 1995 at Stanford University. Larry Page was considering enrolling at Stanford University, and Sergey Brin was a student at
the same university, and he was tasked with taking Larry on a tour of the college.
Some say that they did not agree on almost any topic during their first meeting, but the following year, they formed a partnership. Working from their dorm room, we were able to design a search engine that uses links to determine the importance of individual pages on the World Wide Web. They called that search engine Backrub.
Soon after, they renamed Backrub to Google. The new name was a mathematical expression forthe number 1 followed by 100 zeros, and reflected Larry and Sergey's endeavor to "organize information available in the world and facilitate access to and use of it globally." A few years later,
